## Overview

**Spine Group** is a deep-tech infrastructure company building a closed-loop ecosystem of three interconnected products:

- **Mammoth** — Modular BIO Datacentres that capture and redistribute waste heat from servers. Core IP is a patented "umbilical" delivering power, water, and communications to individual modular pods.
- **Grow** — Vertical farms powered by waste heat captured from Mammoth.
- **Spine** — The connective infrastructure between Mammoth and Grow — a backbone facilitating continuous, mutually beneficial resource flow.

The brand voice is **modern, sophisticated, experimental — Apple meets Nothing.** Cool mad-scientist tech that embraces engineered imperfection at high polish. Visual language draws from architectural drawings, broadsheet typography, and engineering documents. Statements over slogans. Numbers over narrative. The aesthetic is warm-stone neutrals punctuated by a single sharp brand orange used like a scalpel — one hit per composition.

Tagline territory: *"One system. Two revenue streams." / "Performance Through Efficiency."*

## Colors

The palette is warm, earthy, and restrained — punctuated by a sharp brand orange. Solid flat fills only; **no gradients**.

- **Primary (#252525):** Charcoal. Primary dark backgrounds, strong text on light surfaces, button fills. The workhorse dark.
- **Secondary (#1E1F20):** Dark text. Body copy on light surfaces — slightly warmer than pure black.
- **Tertiary (#FF4319):** Warm orange. The brand accent. Used sparingly: CTAs, the single most-important data point on a screen, key annotations. Never decorative — always signaling intent or hierarchy.
- **Accent (#6944E8):** Cool purple. Reserved secondary accent. Used in rare data-visualization or moments of contrast. Avoid pairing with orange in the same composition.
- **Neutral (#EEE9E3):** Stone. The signature warm-light surface — card backgrounds, statement panels, the base canvas of the brand.
- **Surface (#FBF8F5):** Warm white. Page-level light background. Slightly warmer than off-white to keep the brand temperature consistent.
- **Border (#B6B2AD):** Muted warm gray. Hairline rules, divider lines, secondary panel borders.
- **Border Strong (#252525):** Charcoal hairline. Used for emphatic 1px rules between major sections.
- **Muted (#A8A29E):** Annotation text, captions, technical labels. Sits comfortably on both light and dark surfaces.

The orange is **the only chromatic accent that should appear at scale**. Keep purple to data charts, status indicators, or ≤5% of the surface area. Never use both in the same hero.

## Typography

Two typefaces — **KH Teka** for editorial text, **PP Supply Mono** for utility text. Together they evoke a hybrid of broadsheet newspaper and engineering schematic.

**KH Teka** carries the voice. Use it for any display headline, any sentence-case body copy, and any moment where the brand is speaking in full sentences. Tracking is consistently negative (`-0.02em` to `-0.04em`). Display sizes hold at line-height `1.02–1.1`; body text opens up to `1.6`. Long-form body copy is **justified** (text-align: justify) at sizes 14–18px to evoke the density of an investor memo or technical brief.

**PP Supply Mono** carries everything that isn't a sentence: navigation, labels, metadata, page numbers, technical identifiers (`MAM-X23`, `access point`), small-caps tags, and large stylized one-word display moments (e.g. `SPINE` set in Ultralight at 70px). Mono is **always uppercase** in nav and label contexts; tracked open (`+0.05em`). Mono is **never used for body copy.**

Type rhythm:
- **One** display-xl per screen, max.
- Pair display headlines with mono labels above them, never with display subheadings.
- Numbers in display sizes are part of the brand — `278%`, `2×`, `0` are display-tier elements, not body copy.

## Layout

Layouts are built on **strict 1px hairline rules** and **flat planar fills**. The grid is technical: corners register, edges align, gutters are explicit.

- **Base canvas:** 1920×1080 for presentations and product imagery. Web layouts use a 1284px design width and scale.
- **Margins:** 24–32px on the outer frame; 23px top header bar.
- **Section dividers:** 1px solid `{colors.border-strong}` or `{colors.border}` — never colored, never dashed, never decorative. The line itself is the structure.
- **Plus markers (`+`):** Small typographic plus signs at panel corners and section starts — a signature detail borrowed from architectural registration marks. Use PP Supply Mono `+` at the start of section breaks and bottom-right of statement cards.
- **Marginal annotations:** PP Supply Mono labels rotated 90° alongside imagery panels — borrowed from engineering drawings. Use sparingly (one per major composition).
- **Spacing scale** is geometric, doubling roughly every two steps: 4 → 8 → 16 → 24 → 32 → 48 → 64 → 96px. Use the named tokens (`spacing.sm`, `spacing.md`, etc.) — do not introduce intermediate values.

Use `display: grid` with `gap` for any group of sibling elements. Inline flow with whitespace separators is fragile and should be avoided.

## Elevation & Depth

The brand prefers **flat layering** over shadow. Depth is communicated through:

1. **Color contrast** between adjacent panels (charcoal next to stone next to warm-white).
2. **1px hairline rules** at panel boundaries.
3. **Explicit gutters** between cards in a grid (1px gap on a charcoal background).

Drop shadows are minimal and reserved for true overlay surfaces (modals, dropdowns). When used: `0 4px 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.18)`. Never use blurred glow, ambient shadow, or any 3D effect.

## Shapes

**Sharp rectangles only.** No rounded corners on brand panels, statement cards, buttons, inputs, or photography frames.

The only allowed radii:
- `rounded.sm (4px)` — for secondary surfaces in product UI where sharp edges feel hostile (e.g. tooltip).
- `rounded.md (8px)` — for inline UI components inside dense product surfaces.
- `rounded.lg (20px)` — **only** for full-bleed device-frame mockups (representing a physical phone bezel).

Buttons, inputs, cards, navigation, and section panels use `rounded.none`.

## Components

### Buttons

- **`button-primary`** — Charcoal fill, stone text. The default action. Use one per screen as the strongest CTA.
- **`button-accent`** — Orange fill, warm-white text. Reserved for the single most-important conversion action ("Contact", "Get Showreel"). Never two on the same screen.
- **`button-outline`** — Transparent with a 1px charcoal border. The secondary action. Inverts to charcoal-fill on hover.

All buttons use `typography.label-mono` (PP Supply Mono, uppercase, tracked +0.05em). No icons inside buttons. No drop shadows. Hover state reduces opacity to 0.75 — color does not change for primary/accent buttons.

### Inputs

`input-text` is a sharp-cornered field with a 1px charcoal border on the warm-white surface. Focus state replaces the border with `{colors.tertiary}` (orange). Placeholder text is `{colors.muted}`. Form labels above the input use `typography.micro-mono` uppercase.

### Statement Card

The signature Spine Group component. A flat-fill rectangle (stone or charcoal) with:
1. A header bar containing `SPINE GROUP` (PP Supply Mono micro-uppercase) on the left and a `////` dot pattern on the right.
2. A 0.5px hairline rule beneath the header.
3. Display-md or display-sm title in KH Teka.
4. Justified body copy at 11–13px.
5. A **`+`** registration mark in the bottom-right corner.

Use the dark variant (`card-statement-dark`) for hero moments and the light variant for grids of three or more.

### Navigation

Vertical-stacked links anchored top-right of the page, set in `typography.label-mono` (uppercase). Active state and hover state both use `{colors.tertiary}`. The "Showreel" link is permanently orange — it is the brand's standing CTA. No background, no border, no underline; the link text alone carries the affordance.

### Tags

Three variants — outlined (`tag`), filled (`tag-filled`), and accent (`tag-accent`). All sharp corners, all PP Supply Mono uppercase. Used to label products (`MAMMOTH`, `GROW`), states (`NEW`), or technical identifiers (`MAM-X23`).

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do**
- Use the orange like a scalpel — one hit per composition.
- Justify long body copy in KH Teka. The density is part of the voice.
- Set technical identifiers (`MAM-X23`, `access point`) in PP Supply Mono micro-uppercase. They are micro-copy, not chrome.
- Treat numbers as display-tier content. `278%` belongs in a 56px headline.
- Pair photography (B&W, cool, industrial) with stone or charcoal panels — never on warm-white.
- Use `+` registration marks at panel corners and section starts. Use `////` patterns as section separators.

**Don't**
- Use gradients — anywhere. Solid fills only.
- Use rounded corners on brand surfaces. Sharp rectangles are the brand.
- Use emoji. Ever. The brand is purely typographic and photographic.
- Pair orange and purple in the same composition. Choose one accent.
- Use PP Supply Mono for body copy. It is utility text, not voice.
- Use exclamation points or marketing exclamations. The voice is declarative, not promotional.
- Add ambient drop shadows or 3D effects. Layering is achieved through hairlines and color contrast.
- Use icon libraries with filled/colored icons. If an icon is unavoidable, use Lucide single-stroke icons in `{colors.primary}` or `{colors.muted}`.
